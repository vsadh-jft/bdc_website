The General Data Protection Regulation: Everything you need to know.

The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) is a legislation that will affect the way data protection is carried out. Currently, the UK relies on Data Protection Act 1998 but this will be superseded by the new legislation by the end of May 2018. Each company based in the EU, or not based in the EU but working with european customers, will have to abide by the new rules: giving people more control over their personal data and giving businesses a simpler environment in which to operate. The GDPR will apply in all EU member states from 25 May 2018 and the UK will not be exempted.

“Controllers” and “Processors”, that is respectively who states how and why personal data is processed and who does the actual processing of the data, will have to abide by the GDPR once the legislation comes into effect. This involves the fact that once that the purpose is fulfilled and the data is no longer required, it should be deleted.

Controllers must also keep a record of how and when an individual gave consent of processing their data, a consent that can be withdrawn anytime. For this reason the Controllers must carry out changes in the way they collect data from the customers. Furthermore they must allow the customers the access to their personal data at regular intervals. Controllers must respond within one month.  





The companies must especially:

Inform the data protection authority of any data breach within 72 hours of becoming aware of it

Instruct the personnel about the new rules

Adopt a policy governance and data protection which is appropriate to the risk of breach

Introduce a DPO (Data Protection Officer)

Provide themselves with the technology necessary to monitoring and face the possible attacks

The customers can:

Know how their data are used and how they are stored and protected

Ask for the data to be transfered

Be informed in case of breach and theft of their data

Have warranty on the apply of the legislation from the interessed company

Demand the “right to be forgotten”

Violations of the GDPR provide severe fines. Find out how to avoid the risks here.
