Self-Service BI

A new way of planning Business

Self-service BI is a trend with a somewhat vague definition; in the most general sense, self-service BI tasks are those that business users carry out themselves instead of passing them on for fulfillment. The aim is to give to the users of BI tools with more freedom and responsibility at the same time. In its heart lies the notion of user independence and self-sufficiency when it comes to the use of corporate information leading to a decentralization of BI in the organization.
Beyond this rather broad definition, Self-Service BI has many facets; Its meaning depends very much on the precise requirements of user roles because for each one, Self-Service BI can help users accomplish various and specific tasks.


Therefore, the need for self-service within a BI environment varies according to user requirements. They can typically be assigned to three roles which, in reality, overlap and change according to the peculiar task:

  • Casual or standard users make up roughly the 70% of all BI users. Usually, they have a rather limited BI skillset which corresponds with their daily requirements. Therefore, analysis, dynamic reports and dashboards are sufficient to cover their needs in most cases.
  • Power users make up around 25% of all users. They are skilled BI users who need a lot of flexibility and functionality for their daily work to answer their business problems. Suitable self-service tools allow them not only to analyze data but to change existing reports and dashboards from scratch.
  • About 5% of all BI users can be described as business analysts. These are the users with the most advanced BI skills and requirements. They have the highest demand for flexibility and functionality in their solutions. For them, self-service must cover tasks like data exploration, modeling/deploying a sandbox environment for special cases.



Why is Self-Service BI so important?

In many companies, business intelligence is traditionally based on a central Data Warehouse or centrally deployed data marts. However, the methods, architectures and software solutions that have been considered best practices for a long time are now insufficient to meet the growing needs of today’s companies.

One of the reasons why companies increasingly adopt self-service solutions is to the challenge of business departments to have access to data and information anytime and anywhere. To keep ahead of the competition, companies need to act as quickly as possible on new insights gleaned from analytics. Traditional BI delivery models, still suitable for many situations, cannot offer the level of agility and efficiency that is quickly changing requirements demand.

Since both data volumes and sources are growing faster, it is required an iterative approach to find new promising business cases to leverage the full economic value of the data at hand.
As a result, demand for simple, quick and user-friendly BI softwares is still on the rise and has been for years. Enterprises are striving to enable business users to build their queries, reports, interfaces and even data models.

Experts’ New Era

BI business has never been bigger. It should be viewed as an opportunity to implement data-driven decisions improving the way your business does things. However, to successfully deliver your BI solution and achieve real business value, the staff need to be on board from the start and it is necessary to follow three important steps.

  • Step One: Adoption
    It is important giving access to BI because as a whole, it allows your organisation to make better decisions. The key lies in aligning your processes with your people, so they could get the right information to use it in the right way: when people feel as if they are contributing to a project and have the knowledge they need to succeed, they’re more likely to be motivated towards adopting changes and achieve their goals.
  • Step Two: Interpretation
    More your people will be coached on how to accurately interpret the data presented to them, the more effective your BI solutions will be. For a successful performance, you need to know business inside out; without being able to interpret the data correctly, there’s a much less chance of preventing future failings or issues.
  • Step Three: Application
    The final step is the application of your BI solution. An effective solution allows for improved business processes – your organisation’s raw data must be gathered so that it maintains progress towards achieving your goals. As well as building an ever-evolving process model, BI should allow you to discover the bigger business picture; a canvas where involvement and strong awareness are very crucial.

    To obtain the most out of the BI it needs to be clear: ex-ante, what you want to get out, then the goals you put in mind and finally the way you want to get there. In simple terms the key to success is represented by the efficient match between software and workforce, made possible through a continuous process of quality and speed’s improvement to enable you to get an answer to any problem by allowing major productivity and efficiency.
