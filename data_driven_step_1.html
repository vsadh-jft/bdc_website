Data Driven Organisation: Step 1.0

Intro
Every day, the world creates 2,500 petabytes of data. In the past two years, mankind has generated more data than in the preceding 5,000 years combined.

For some it’s early, for others it’s already too late. On the road to becoming data-driven, it’s crucial for people to change their mindset and for organisations to change their processes, because gut feeling is not good enough anymore to differentiate yourself from your competitors: if you want to stay in the wake of the wave, Real Time Data Driven Analytics is the path you have to travel.

Indeed, Data-Driven organisations that are already getting in line with time and technological progress use and process ever more data to improve and speed up their decision-making because the goal of having superior analytics is always having superior insights in order to better understand the real outline of the situation.


Machines and things are generating a constant flux of information which is transforming our world. Companies have now to understand that everything around us has turned into data and by now decisions that aren’t supported by them, are considered suspicious or at least lacking of concrete bedrock.

Just to mention a concrete example, Swisscom - Switzerland’s biggest Telecom operator, found that the analysis of their customer service data was too slow and required too much manual work; It wasn’t so useful for customers, then through smarter text analytics, relationships and possible solutions were shown faster and faster, until becoming often almost simultaneous  as the ongoing call center documentation evolved. Customer Service Data is now processed 7 times faster, making it far more useful in issue solving. “We are able to create fully automated daily reports, which has a direct positive effect on service quality and customer satisfaction.” Added Albert Labermeier, Senior Marketing Analyst at Swisscom.

Improved performances, more Real Time profitability, stronger innovations: all these benefits are clear for the healthcare of a company that want to put itself back on its feet, but there are still some technical challenges to overcome:




Unstructured Data - they are about 80% of the all amount; they aren’t predefined and doesn’t fit the mould of traditional data models such as text documents, pictures, e-mails, sensor data, and much more. This data is hard to analyse for traditional analytics programs, although it contains valuable insights.

Unconnected Systems - Organisations often use multiple information storage systems side by side but sometimes these systems may even offer conflicting information because of different sources, processing methods or naming conventions in a classic problem of aggregation.

Low Data Quality - Sometimes, the data quality simply isn’t good enough, because of poor data input or poorly implemented data connections. In these cases it’s hard to get good Business Intelligence from poor – or plain wrong – Data.

Misaligment With IT - Business units shouldn’t have to depend on IT for data analytics, they should be able to run it themselves. With IT being under constant pressure to keep delivering more at lower costs, your data analytics requests may end up at the bottom of their list.

All of these challenges can be overcome by defining a roadmap towards better data analytics solutions.

Companies that want to stay ahead must become data-driven decision making.
