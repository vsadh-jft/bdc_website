The Big Data Revolution

Nowadays it isn’t rare to hear about Big Data, but who of you could really explain what it means? We believe that it is fair enough to be informed about it, and we are here to explain it to you.

Big Data represents an evolution for many sectors, especially for the marketing. For this reason all the companies should know what we are talking about, in order to not fall behind the competition. 2018 has started and the quantity of data which is distributed each day is huge and keeps increasing: we know this is the best opportunity for the companies, since that they can be able to use, process and analyse this information.

Many organisations have already started to use Big Data for their business, Netflix is one of them. It uses namely Data, by mean of algorithms, to offer to customers better experiences of purchase. This phenomenon is possible because, while we surf the net, third parts store our Data: earlier purchases, researches and reviews. This is why we find on the Internet commercials which seem to be studied especially for us.  





There are however some obstacles: Data is not always available to everyone or shared, especially across the centres of research. This often happens in the medical field where the original insight of a research is only shared with the team of research, even though this could save some lives.

The use of Big Data by the way is more than this, a good use of it could lead to many improvements:

• a faster dispatch of the police, better aware of where and how many crimes are going to take place

• faster and better medical, environmental researches

• faster analysis of the weather

• progress of the number of infected individuals in a determined region

We at the Business D Consulting believe that’s a big mistake for companies not having a good system of analytics. It is also strictly important to identify the sources of Data they use, so that they can incorporate them in their platform of Data Management. Moreover, not all the information is needed to you, this means that if you don’t want to find yourself digging in the mountain of Data you have available, it’s always better to have someone who manages it. Keep it in mind: nowadays everything is possible thanks to modern technology.

Final aim of your data analytics is to grant meaning to the information: your model of Data analysis have to change in a more descriptive, predictive and prescriptive way, in order to generate insights, or, in other words knowledge of your customers: their needs, their will, their hearts.
